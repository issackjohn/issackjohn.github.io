Oncology ICU Rounds Prep

OncoICU Rounds Studio is a small clinical-prep application I built to explore Google’s MedGemma and Gemma models in a real product setting. The idea was to see how well an open, medically-tuned model could support preparing for oncology ICU rounds — organizing patient context and surfacing the right things to review before walking into the unit.

It walks through a four-step flow — Case Room → Pre-round → Rounds → Debrief — built around a library of synthetic ICU cases (no PHI). You pick a teaching lens and a learner role, work up the chart yourself, then check your reasoning against the model. It was as much an exploration of the models as it was of the app: where MedGemma is genuinely helpful, where it needs guardrails, and what a responsible interface around a medical model should actually look like.

Live demo →

Why I built it

I wanted hands-on intuition for the MedGemma/Gemma family beyond a notebook — to understand what it feels like to put a medical model behind a real UI, with real latency, real prompts, and the real responsibility that comes with anything clinical. Rounds prep is a concrete, high-context task, which made it a good forcing function.

Stack

  • Google MedGemma and Gemma for the model layer.
  • React + Vite front end.
  • Deployed on Cloudflare Pages.

Interface

What I took away

The interesting problems were never just “call the model.” They were about context, trust, and restraint — how much to show, how to keep a medical model from overstepping, and how to make the output something a person could actually rely on. It’s an exploration, not a clinical tool, but it taught me a lot about building seriously around open medical models.

References

  • MedGemma — Google DeepMind’s medically-tuned Gemma variant.
  • MedGemma 1.5 & MedASR — next-generation medical image interpretation and speech-to-text.
  • Gemma 4 — the open base model family.
